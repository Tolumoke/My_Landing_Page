The Problem

Most projects don't fail at construction.
They fail before the first column is cast.

"By the time a cost overrun appears in your monthly report, it is already 3–4 weeks old. The damage was done in silence."
01

Contracts are signed without reading the risk. Bills of Quantities, provisional sums, and escalation clauses hide multi-million-naira exposures. All of it legal. All of it preventable.

02

Monitoring systems look backwards. Monthly reports describe what already happened. By the time you read a problem, correction costs twice as much as prevention would have.

03

Decisions are made without intelligence. The developers, contractors, and financiers who lose the most are not unintelligent. They are uninformed at the exact moment a decision is made.

About
Senior Planning & Cost Control Engineer

12 years of detecting what numbers are trying to say.

I am Tolumoke Israel Ola, a Construction Cost and Risk Intelligence Engineer with over a decade of field experience across building and highway infrastructure projects in Nigeria.

I have worked on projects from the ground up; sitting in site offices, reading equipment logs, interrogating cost reports, and building the systems that flag problems before they become crises. Across 12 years and over $7 million in documented savings, I have learned one thing above all: the data is always trying to tell you something. The question is whether you have a system that can hear it.

I am currently applying data science, causal AI, and systems thinking to construction cost intelligence; building the diagnostic tools that the next generation of Nigerian infrastructure projects will need to stop repeating the same expensive mistakes.
